Game Modes

Three ways to practice,
one goal: get faster.

01

Standard

20 questions, 3 lives. A focused session with a clear endpoint — like a workout set. Track your score, streak, and accuracy across the round.

  • Questions20
  • Lives3
  • Best forDaily warm-up
02

Infinite

No end — just a steady stream of problems until you run out of lives. This is where real fluency builds. Keep your streak alive as long as you can.

  • Questions∞
  • Lives3
  • Best forDeep practice
03

Timed

60 seconds, no lives, every second counts. The clock changes how you think — you learn to trust your instant recall rather than counting through each step.

  • Duration60s
  • LivesUnlimited
  • Best forPressure training

Why It Works

Mental math is a skill,
not a talent.

Spaced repetition

Every session mixes operations randomly, forcing your brain to retrieve the right procedure each time — the mechanism that builds long-term recall.

Progressive overload

Difficulty scales from 1–10 to 1–500. As you master one level, the next pushes your working memory further — just like progressive weight training.

Immediate feedback

You know right away whether you were right or wrong — and what the correct answer was. That instant correction loop is what drives improvement.

Operations

Pick your — or pick all three.

+

Addition

The foundation. Quick addition fluency makes subtraction and multiplication easier by extension.

−

Subtraction

Often the hardest for beginners. Requires holding the minuend while computing the difference.

×

Multiplication

Capped at 20×20 to stay within mental range. Drills the times tables until they’re automatic.
